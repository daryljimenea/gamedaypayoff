Parlay Odds

A Parlay is a single wager that links together two or more individual wagers and is dependent on all of those wagers winning together.  For more on what a Parlay is, refer to Parlay Wagers in the Wager Types section of the Help.

This screen is not accessible if Parlays are not being offered and is demonstrated as such in the Parlay Rules tab.  If it is offered, the odds for each Parlay are reflected in the table by "Win" and "Bet".  It is important to note that if a field is blank, that Parlay is not offered.  Additionally, you cannot skip plays being offered.  There cannot be a gap in offerings for plays.  Example:  you cannot have a 2, 3, and then 5 play with no 4 play offered.  The main reason for this is if there is a push and the odds are reduced to the next lowest number, it would not work if it was not offered.  For instance, in the example, if it was a 5 play parlay and there was a push or no action with one of the plays, there would be no 4 play to drop the odds to.  Since this scenario cannot feasibly be handled, it is not allowed.

Parlays are designated in the Wager Manager by Parlay (#plays).  They are available to all sports and plays within the same Parlay may come from different sports.
