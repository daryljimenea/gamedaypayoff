Pleaser Odds

A Pleaser is a group of spread bets or totals combined into one wager whereby each play has points moved against you.  For more on what a Pleaser is, refer to Pleaser Wagers in the Wager Types section of the Help.

This screen is not accessible if you choose not to offer them in the Pleaser Rules tab.  If it is offered, the odds for each Pleaser are reflected in the table by "Win" and "Bet".  Each odds table has points assigned to them.  Make sure to check each points dropdown for pre-populated odds before assigning them to your Players.

It is important to note that if a field is blank, that Pleaser is not offered.  Additionally, you cannot skip plays being offered.  There cannot be a gap in offerings for plays.  Example:  you cannot have a 2, 3, and then 5 play with no 4 play offered.  The main reason for this is if there is a push and the odds are reduced to the next lowest number, it would not work if it was not offered.  For instance, in the example, if it was a 5 play pleaser and there was a push or no action with one of the plays, there would be no 4 play to drop the odds to.  Since this scenario cannot feasibly be handled, it is not allowed.

Pleasers are designated in the Wager Manager by Pleaser (#plays),(#points).  At this time, they are only available to football.  However, plays within a Pleaser may be mixed between college and pro.
